tomewing:

There’s nothing wrong with stopping seeking out the new and idealising the old, by the way. I mean, as long as you don’t do it for everything you care about, that’s unhealthy. But it’s a big life and you have less and less of it, triage is going to happen. Just don’t be a dick to your ex-interests, or more to the point, to the people who share them.

Totally. The problem is “Music is shitty now” vs “I don’t really like new music for reasons” where reasons might be lack of time, energy, or just taste – I don’t listen to a bunch of new music anymore because I’m increasingly aware that I may not be able to read every single book ever written, they keep writing more!

That said, I think there are also environmental factors in any media ecosystem which might mean that more or less good work is able to be produced in a given medium at a given time – I mean a certain type of novel flourished in the 18th & 19th centuries because a) increasing literacy b) lack of television and otehr stuff to do after work… or this last decade has seen real boom in the quality and availability of boardgames cos what was once a diffuse market has been made accessible via the internet etc. etc.
