“a long piece of prose with something wrong with it”—

Often attributed to Neil Gaiman but as this post of his from July 2001 makes clear it wasn’t him who originally said it.

I pretty much agree with this as a definition of a novel, or at least I novel that I’m likely to like. Something being “wrong” is important, it shows overreaching, an attempt to go beyond what should be/ is possible. This isn’t true of short stories though. Borges, Ballard and Kelly Link all regularly write/ wrote perfect short stories. Ursula Le Guin can too.

“AMBIGUITY SHOULDN’T FEEL LIKE IT’S BEING DECLARED TO THE AUDIENCE … IT SHOULD FEEL LIKE IT’S BEING EXPLORED WITH THE AUDIENCE.”—

Film Crit Hulk Smash: THE DAMON LINDELOF INTERVENTION | Badass Digest

Reading a bunch of stuff about Prometheus. For all its faults (this by Genevieve Valentine seems to me a pretty definitive list, an analysys from within the genric conventions of sci-fi, rather than the ‘hulk’ piece which looks at its failings film qua film) anyway, for all the films failings it does that thing that Lost did which is to very effectively push certain nerd buttons, setting up an itch that can only be scratched by all sorts of crazy unwarranted extrapolation c.f that Space Jesus nonsense. Part of this for me is external to the film: the feeling that “what, these people spent a gazillon dollars making something that’s totally riddled with contradictions and inconsistencies? no way would anyone spend all that money on something so stupid”. Further, the feeling that “I was excited about it and now feel a bit embarrassed about that in the light of the terrible mess that I’ve just watched and have I wasted my life? So anyway I’m going to try to figure out some way to explain it all so I don’t feel so bad* ”. 

I know I need to leave it alone now but if you’re unlucky enough to join me in the pub in the near future I’m sure I’ll complain about it at boring length. Probably followed by going on about Primer at boring length.

BTW: The above quotation pretty much encapsulates the problem with the final shot of Inception.

* this must be even more acute in the case of Lost, (how many hours 120 or so?)  I think I saw that it was heading in this messy direction pretty quickly and jumped off after episode 4 or 5.

my reply to "Adventures in the kitchen: experiments #1, #2, #3 and #4"

5) Do herbs like basil and parsley add anything? I can tell when there’s cilantro in a meal, but the other herbs seem far too subtle to bother with. Should I be doing something to unlock their flavour?

YES! Both are amazing.

Basil’s main use in our house is pesto. If you like garlic and want something simple and quick then home made pesto is your friend. Note: toasting the pine nuts is important, I was too lazy to do this for a while but it never worked out right.

Otherwise basil’s good with mozzarella and tomato salad (sliced tomatoes, mozarella, basil leaves, salt, olive oil, balsamic), anything with tomatoes can be improved by basil.

Parsley there’s a couple of types: Flat leaf is in some ways similar to coriander (cilantro) in appearance and taste and again is good in salads - tabouleh a staple at our place. Curly leaf parsley is traditionally used in white sauces and with fish and stuff I’m not such a fan but it has it’s uses.

I’m on the lookout for recipes that are fast and flavourful. Ideas?

My ultimate quick meal for one: Melt a chunk of butter in a pan until it goes a golden add 5 or 6 sage leaves and keep it warm. Then mix this up with a load of cooked pasta (linguine is my preference, also for pesto) and sprinkle with some parmesan, maybe some lemon juice. Whole thing takes about 5 minutes.
