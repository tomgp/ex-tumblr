Kurt Vonnegut - Top 14

Charlie asked me on twitter:

@tomp Out of the blue! You wrote a blog entry about a million years ago listing your favourite Vonneguts in decreasing order of favouritism.

That old entry - and the blog within which it was contained - is now history* so here’s what I reckon now, it’s probably different from what I reckoned then. I’ve only included novels. In brackets I’ve added the grade KV awarded himself in the 1981 Palm Sunday collection (IMO the best collection of his non fiction (which also includes some fiction)), I think he was a pretty good judge of his own work:

Excellent:

  • Galápagos
  • Cat’s Cradle (A+)
  • Slaughterhouse-Five (A+)
  • Bluebeard
  • Mother Night (A, though I’ve seen a TV interview where he revised this to A+)
  • Hocus Pocus

Good:

  • The Sirens of Titan (A)
  • Jailbird (A)
  • Deadeye Dick
  • Player Piano (B)
  • God Bless You, Mr. Rosewater (A)

Pretty good:

  • Breakfast of Champions ©
  • Slapstick (D)
  • Timequake

PS: There’s a couple of Library of America collections: Novels and Stories ‘63-'73 and Novels and Stories '50-'62 both of which strike me as excellent value and which I would recommend to anyone who likes excellent things.

*there is an SQL dump of it somewhere but that’s more trouble than it’s worth for this

“And Mulius next— he reared and jammed his lance through the man’s ear so the lance came jutting out through the other ear, bronze point glinting”—

240: Achilles kills Mulius

Homer, The Iliad book 20. Translated by Robert Fagles

(via deathsoftheiliad)

I’ve got about another fortnight of Iliad Deaths to do, the whole thing has given me even imenser respect for Phil Gyford and his Pepys’ Diary project.

I guess I could have chosen a digitally available translation, from project Guttenberg or something, so I didn’t need to transcribe each act of slaughter by hand - in fact I intended to vary translations though out - but Fagles’ version really is the freshest.

I mean compare the above with the same bit from the Samuel Butler version on guttenberg.

“Achilles then went up to Mulius and struck him on the ear with a spear, and the bronze spear-head came right out at the other ear.”

I have no idea re faithfulness or whatever but Butlers’ rendering is just kind of awkward.

But yeah, as I was saying, I could have gone for Butler’s version or some other easy cut and paste job but even so, just having to keep up with a daily schedule (in practice enqueuing a bunch of stuff every week or two) has been challenging, I mean some days I had to forego several hours of Browsing Tumblr’s Game Of Thones tag or whatever.

Not sure what I’m going to do with the blog once done. First tidy up all the tags I guess then maybe start on the Odyssey or something? Possible make a kind of death score card or something…

So, in summary I barely have the discipline to complete even fairly basic things if they extend for more than a couple of months.

“A lifetime of data has proven to [Romney] that he’s extraordinarily, even uniquely, good at managing and leading organizations, projects and people,” Klein writes. “It’s those skills, rather than specific policy ideas, that he sees as his unique contribution. Versus here:

“The Obama campaign’s data was centralized and coordinated, while the Romney campaign relied in large part on SuperPACs which by law could not have access to the central database and could not be coordinated.

SuperPACs are dumb money. Their cash can almost never be effectively spent, because they’re not on the inside of the campaign. What’s more, because they’re not official campaigns, they always pay top dollar for their TV ad spots, rather than the discounted rates that stations are forced to offer to candidates. The Obama campaign determined, at various points, that if they approached potential donors with the message that Romney had a fundraising lead, that would help Obama raise more money for his own campaign. But the truth was that Romney’s fundraising lead was never particularly useful, because it wasn’t married to a coherent strategy and database. If anything, it just helped Obama raise more cash.”
