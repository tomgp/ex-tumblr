Cullen at Greenside

leftoverlondoner:

Gordon Cullen’s mural at Greenside Primary School in west London was on proud display during the recent Open House London weekend. Erno Goldfinger designed the school buildings and turned to friend Cullen to brighten things up with an educational mural in the entrance area. It is a lovely thing, very much of its 1950s era and redolent of Ladybird Books, Janet & John neat haircuts and brave new learning.

I wonder how many thousands of kids have been inspired by Cullen’s work over the last 60-something years?

Thursday, October 15, 2015

@drefenn said  :

Wow I am so impressed that you are still persisting!

Halfway(ish) report: yeah, my submissions are maybe getting more er… perfuntory though.

Interestingly I’ve been drawing a lot more, at lunch time and in the evening, but it tends to be messy pages full of smudged sketches and doodles so then I have to turn some of that into something that I can take a photo of and other people have a chance of identifying.

it’s kind of reminding me of the role of boredom and repetition in creative production (my own at least), like imagine drawing the same thing over and over again and after a while you just have to try new things or look for new subjects or you find something you like and repeat it and exagerate it (e.g. I like the bottle’s shaddow in this one, the hatching suggesting the wood grain of the table, I want to explore that kind of thing more) c.f. that link I posted the other day about how “now that, generally, one need not wait for things, patience becomes an active and positive cognitive state” – I’m really taken by that idea.

Also, the limitations and affordances of ink (with respect to myself), like that trilobite fossil didn’t come out so well because it’s basically a flat surface with really subtle degrees of shadow all over it and I’m not good enough to get that right, but for drawing people it works well with my natural tendency towards bold straightish lines.

So yeah, I’m sticking with it, though reserve the right to flake out at any moment.
