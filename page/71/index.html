innowheres:

Wolf Hall was pretty decent, eh?

Yeah, the staging and casting was great – though Damien Lewis looks too small in spite of the best efforts of the camera angles. It felt to me pretty faithful but also really different to the source (obviously, inevitably because its a 6 part TV series rather than the first two novels in a trilogy); really sparse when the books are so dense and byzantine. I guess we were always going to miss Cromwell’s mental machinations and weighing up of events and so on. But yeah, I’ll be watching the rest.

cf I Claudius book vs TV prog.

Aside: The BBC seem desperate to make it a big event with historians talking about it on News Night (which I didn’t watch) and a hashtag (which I didn’t follow) and so on but the actual thing felt pretty low key (in a good way).

“Actually, the suburbs are far more sinister places than most city dwellers imagine. Their very blandness forces the imagination into new areas. I mean, one’s got to get up in the morning thinking of a deviant act, merely to make certain of one’s freedom. It needn’t be much; kicking the dog will do.”

—

A JG Ballard interview in The Paris Review from the early-mid 80s. Lots of stuff about the writing process and routine and so on (“Actually, there’s no secret. One simply pulls the cork out of the bottle, waits three minutes, and two thousand or more years of Scottish craftsmanship does the rest.”).

(Also, the synopsis of high-rise – written in the form of a social workers report of course – was longer than the final novel.)
