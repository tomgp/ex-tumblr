American publishers put out significantly fewer works in translation in 2015.

aintgotnoladytronblues:

toffeemilkshake:

millionsmillions:

Recommended Reading: The largest publishers of translated works in the United States published less in 2015 than they normally do—503 works of fiction down from 597 from the previous year.

Looking at the database to which they link (I may be reading the collection of spreadsheets wrong, only a cursory glance etc.) the figures as currently tallied seem to be.

2008, 360
2009, 363
2010, 340
2011, 370
2012, 460
2013, 541
2014, 600
2015, 549

Which suggests a classic regression to mean type situation to me i.e. after the peak year of 2014 things have gone back a bit but there’s still a general growth. I’m always suspiscious of claims for a dramatic change in a set of figures which don’t place those figures in a historical context. Like: if the figures were 600,601,598,603,549 then that certainly more likely to be an actual thing but there’s clearly quite a lot of variation in the figures (or an inconsistency in measument/ collection)

None of which is to say that it wouldn’t be nice to get more writers translated.
