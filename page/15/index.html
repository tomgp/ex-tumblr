“But how awful to see society embrace art that makes you feel nothing, that makes you think only about the vast chasm between you and everyone else.”

—

Dan Brooks, “Banksy and the Problem with Sarcastic Art,” NY Times (via wilfordlauren)

I haven’t been to Dismaland (everyone who I’ve spoken to who has has raved about it) and I tend, generally, to be a bit eye-rolling about Banksy but it strikes me that most of the articles critiquing Dismaland tend to brush over or ignore the fact that the show isn’t all Banksy, there are like 70-odd different artists of varying degrees of fame from various positions on the gallery-street specturm – to treat the whole thing as homogeneous is strange. Also; that Jonathan Jones disliked it is probably the best endorsement I can imagine.
