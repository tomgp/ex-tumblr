Sunday, April 12, 2015

Yes the hugo’s malarkey has roots older than the gamergate malarkey and it would be disingenuous to suggests that the awards had not previously been ‘politicised’ – as is any area of human endeavour esp. where money, prestige etc are at stake. But recent events are a clear escalation, moving things from the ranty-blog-post-and-vitriolic-comments-section level to the physical-threats-and-actual-harassment level – that this upgrade in hostilities has been linked explicitly to gamergate by the people who the put together the list of things to vote for together can come as no surprise to any one who’s been watching the whole horrific gg thing unfold.

Aside: it feels like for a while the whole hugo’s process has been moving from direct democracy to a kind of ad-hoc party politics (which I suspect is the worst kind of party politics) and this latest furore represents a kind of phase shift.

Saturday, April 11, 2015

Back from Wales. Catching up on the Internet – the main news in my bit of it seems to be how gamergate has metastasised over to sci-fi fandom, most visibly in terms of block voting to get the Hugo award lists stuffed full of books by mysoginists and white supremacists (one of the nominees is literally published by “patriarchy press”) less visibly there’s been some cheerful reading about people (women) having SWAT teams called out to their houses.

Who are these people who look at the history of science fiction over the last 40 years and more and think “you know what? this whole think would be better without all the women messing things up”? No Le Guin, no Joanna Russ or Margret Atwood, no Tricia Sullivan, Kelly Link, Octavia Butler, Mary Shelley, Karen Joy Fowler, Dorris Lessing or Lauren Beukes? I mean. Just?


(I’m guessing some people who read this have managed to avoid knowing about the online hate mob “gamergate” so far, that’s for the best)

Now to catch up on election stuff, can’t imagine I’ll be enraged by anything on that topic.
