Rhetorical question: Is it acceptable for a co-worker (who I don’t know and have never spoken to) to dump the contents of his coffee mug into the plant sitting on the end of my desk?

Actual question: What should I do? I feel like it might be getting off on the wrong foot to be like “Hey! Would you mind not dumping your dregs in my plant?” but on the the other hand the kitchen is only about 15 paces further on… it’s been going on for a couple of weeks now, he’s always in and out quickly, so I haven’t really got a chance to say anything before he’s off again so I’d have to shout at his back as he quickly retreats to his desk seems like a particularly aggressive way to enter a dialogue. Despite my irritation I don’t want this guy who apparently hasn’t learned basic mug washing skills to feel bad or embarrassed about his behaviour.

Maybe I should just start leaving food wrappers on the end of his desk. Probably the best thing to do is complain about it on Tumblr and then just do nothing.

Actually, think I might move the plant closer to my computer, maybe the slight extra effort required and increased visibility will deter the dumper
