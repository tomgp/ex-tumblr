Mad Max: Not Your Fight, Not Your Victory

wilfordlauren:

The following is a guest essay/review/ramble about the Mad Max series by my fiancé Ryan Stevenson, a teacher/writer/filmmaker with a lot of thoughts about the movies that he mostly keeps to himself. He free-wrote his thoughts today after our viewing of Fury Road, and I thought they…

I really liked this. Probably closest thing I’ve read to some of my own thoughts re this new Mad Max film esp. w.r.t. it’s relationship to the series as a whole. How Max is never really central and just kind of agrees to help out for a variety of reasons and often as not messes things up.

Other things I thought: (apropos of nothing) The first Mad Max film has always seemed like more of a horror film than an action film to me, in the tradition of of the kind of unvarnished reality horror of e.g. Witchfinder General.

I kind of missed the To Build A Fire like bits from the earlier films – there was a glimpse of that kind of thing in the opening scene but ultimately the film realy benefitted from the kind of ruthless focus on racing, fighting, exploding.

Also I kind of missed the Australian-ness of the first 3 films, like the Australian equivalent of what 80’s 2000AD is for britishness. I’m always really into Australian nuclear paranoia/apocalypse stuff key work: Victor Kelleher’s Taronga.
