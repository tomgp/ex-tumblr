What is code? If you don't know, you need to read this:

The world belongs to people who code. Those who don’t understand will be left behind.
Paul Ford

So I read this whole magazine article (it’s the whole of businessweek this week) that’s like 35000(~+/-) words long – i was suspiscious of whether this would be a good use of my time. It’s pretty good. Paul Ford is a good writter. It’s basically got three interleaved bits:

  1. an explanation of what is involved, mechanically speaking, in writing computer programs, that kind of segues into an overview of popular/ historically important programing languages – good stuff insofar as I agree with the points made and can see it would be genuinely helpful in understanding all that crap.

  2. kind of moving up the chain from programing languages themselves to the communities that exist around them, conferences etc. and the sort of meta communities like Stack Overflow and so on, how those thing interact and how they inform development & design methodologies &c. This is probably the bit I thought was most interesting, I think the cultrural environment in which programming/coding takes place is not really examined enough, this is a good start.

  3. Then interleaved with this is a sort of office story about making some software which leavens the whole thing, illustrates some of the points made in 1 and 2 and generally provides the macro structure for the piece. There’s a bit of a management book vibe to this part, if you have to ever had to read a management book for a proffesional studies course at uni, or maybe you were really bored at an exeptoinally poorly stocked airport bookshop, some of the tropes here will be familiar (heroic manager fails to understand some thing about people he has to manage then learns some lessons and does some sucessfull managing: Bonus! Promotion! Respect of peers!) – i guess that makes sense for the typical Business Week reader. Personally I’d have liked that bit, bit 3, to be a more critical nd less generic buiness parable. I mean most software projects fail and this isn’t really disucussed, that’s a big cultural feature of software development.

So, yes, probably worth reading though I don’t think you’re going to be left behind if you don’t (as the strapline up there suggests) so don’t worry too much (also, read it on your phone or you’ll be constantly distracted by glitchy gimicks (some of which are actually pretty cool) (though on iOS safari the footnotes don’t work))
